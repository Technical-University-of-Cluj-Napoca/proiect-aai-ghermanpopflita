WHO Clinical Protocols for the Management of Coma and Altered Consciousness
Document Code: WHO/MNH/NEUR.2024.3
Management of Altered Consciousness and Coma:
Assessment of altered consciousness must begin with airway, breathing, and circulation (ABC). Neurological evaluation utilizes the Glasgow Coma Scale (GCS).
Glasgow Coma Scale:
- Eye opening: 1 (none) to 4 (spontaneous)
- Verbal response: 1 (none) to 5 (oriented)
- Motor response: 1 (none) to 6 (obeys commands)
GCS categorization:
- Mild: 13-15
- Moderate: 9-12
- Severe (Coma): <= 8
Immediate escalation is mandatory for GCS <= 8 (requires endotracheal intubation to protect the airway), sudden pupil asymmetry, or signs of herniation. Hypoglycemia must be ruled out by checking capillary blood glucose in all patients.
Evidence Level: A
