EAN/EFNS Guidelines on the Diagnostic Evaluation of Coma and Altered States of Consciousness
European Academy of Neurology (EAN) Guidelines
Diagnostic Workflow:
Altered states of consciousness (ASC) include coma, vegetative state, and minimally conscious state.
Immediate evaluation includes:
1. Neurological exam: Pupils (size, symmetry, response to light), brainstem reflexes (corneal, oculocephalic), motor response to pain.
2. Glasgow Coma Scale (GCS) scoring.
3. Diagnostic testing: Head CT (rule out hemorrhage, mass lesion, herniation), EEG (rule out non-convulsive status epilepticus), Lumbar Puncture (if meningitis/encephalitis suspected and no mass lesion on CT).
4. Lab testing: Metabolic panel, arterial blood gas, toxicology screen (drug levels, opioids, benzodiazepines).
Airway protection (endotracheal intubation) is indicated for GCS <= 8.
Evidence Level: B
